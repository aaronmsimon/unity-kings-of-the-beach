### Idle Animation Ideas for Menu Select
[ ] Look around — slow head turn left, then right, taking in the surroundings
[ ] Neck roll — full slow neck rotation to loosen up
[ ] Volleyball toss — flip the ball up with one hand, let it come back down, catch it
[ ] Shoulder stretch — pull one arm across the chest, hold, switch sides
[ ] Dig practice — drop into a low ready stance, mime a forearm dig, reset
[ ] Sand toe drag — glance down, drag one foot slowly through the sand
[ ] Wrist shake-out — shake both wrists loose like warming up before a game
[ ] Spin the ball — hold the ball in both hands and give it a slow spin, studying it
[ ] Quad stretch — lift one foot behind them, grab the ankle, hold for a beat
[ ] Sun squint — raise a hand to shade their eyes and look off into the "distance"
[ ] Hair/headband adjustment — reach up and fix their hair or adjust a headband
[ ] Deep breath — visible inhale, chest rises, slow exhale, shoulders drop
[ ] Calf raises — rise up on tiptoes twice, subtle but lively
[ ] Ball bounce — bounce the volleyball off the ground once or twice and catch it
[ ] Overhead reach — arms stretch up overhead, slight lean to each side
[ ] Confident lean — shift weight to one hip, cross arms loosely, glance sideways
[ ] Ball roll on forearm — balance the ball rolling along one forearm for a moment
[ ] Serve shadow swing — step back and go through a slow-motion overhand serve motion (no ball)
[ ] Yawn and recover — a small yawn they try to hide, then straighten back up alertly
[ ] Check nails / inspect hand — casually hold one hand up and glance at it, relaxed and a bit cocky

### Randomize Clips
- No synchronization weirdness — if all four athletes played the same long sequence, they'd be in lockstep. Random clips means they're always doing different things at different times, which makes the screen feel alive.
- Easy to add/remove — you can drop in a new clip or pull one out without touching a big sequence.
- Weighted randomization — you can make neutral idles (look around, breathe) play more frequently than the flashier ones (ball toss, serve shadow swing) so it doesn't feel hyperactive.
- Per-athlete personality — later on if you want, you could give certain athlete archetypes a pool that skews toward specific clips (the cocky one gets "check nails" more often, the focused one does more stretch/warmup animations).
